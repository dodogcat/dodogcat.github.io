## Yunseok Jang

I am an engineer focused on physics-based simulation, numerical validation, and performance profiling.  
My work sits around simulation implementation, GPU optimization, engine integration, and system validation rather than a single narrow domain.

### Focus Areas

- **Physics-based Simulation**: MPM simulator design and GPU kernel implementation
- **Performance and Reliability**: bottleneck analysis, numerical stability, reproducibility
- **Engine and System Integration**: Unity, Unreal Engine, on-device inference pipelines
- **Validation-oriented Development**: coordinate alignment, repeated experiments, external-system linkage

### Highlights

- **Validation Efficiency**: improved repeated experiment efficiency and stability through model and computation-flow refinement
- **13% Kernel Speedup**: improved a bottleneck kernel through atomic reduction, kernel fusion, and memory hierarchy optimization
- **On-device AI Profiling**: identified NMS bottlenecks in YOLOv8 / OWL-ViT deployment pipelines
- **Engine Transfer**: ongoing MPM transfer into UE5 Niagara HLSL Custom Modules

### How I Work

I care less about making a system run once and more about whether it can be explained, measured, and trusted.

That usually means answering three questions:

1. **Is the model stable enough to use?**
2. **Can the bottleneck be explained with measured evidence?**
3. **Does the system remain usable after integration with surrounding modules?**

During my master's program, I worked on MPM-based granular simulation and handled physical modeling, numerical validation, and performance profiling together. I also worked on Unity-based vision pipelines, Quest 2 interaction systems, and CAD/FDS-linked visualization workflows, which strengthened my ability to connect algorithms with usable software systems.
